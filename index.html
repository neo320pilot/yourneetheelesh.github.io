The Beginning

From the Island of Mauritius
to the Skies of Ireland

Neetheelesh Ramkissoon was born on the 13th of April, 1980, in the coastal suburb of Fond du Sac, Mauritius — a place of warm trade winds and open horizons that would quietly shape the trajectory of his life. From an early age, the sky was not a boundary but a beckoning.

His formal flight training began at Western Airport, Dublin, Ireland, where he earned his wings and took his first decisive steps into professional aviation. It was here that a lifelong calling was confirmed — not merely as a profession, but as a way of being.

He has crossed oceans alone at the controls of various general aviation aircraft — adventures that were as breathtaking as they were humbling.

The Journey

Crossing Oceans,
Delivering Wings

Upon obtaining his licence, Neetheelesh embarked on one of aviation's most demanding and storied pursuits — the delivery of light aircraft across the globe. Flying a Cessna 172, he navigated vast stretches of open ocean, remote coastlines, and unfamiliar airspace, threading his way across continents with little more than skill, preparation, and unwavering resolve.

The experiences were extraordinary — marked by moments of profound beauty and, at times, genuine peril. Each crossing deepened his mastery of the aircraft, sharpened his situational awareness, and forged the character of a pilot who had truly earned his place in the sky. His passion for flying, and the depth of experience he accumulated across those years, became well known among those who shared his world.

Notable Incident

Calm Under Pressure —
2014 Engine Failure

In 2014, Neetheelesh demonstrated the full measure of his training and composure when he successfully landed a Boeing 737 following an in-flight engine failure. With the lives of all those on board in his hands, he executed the emergency procedures with precision and calm — bringing the aircraft down safely without incident.

It is the kind of moment that defines a pilot's character — not the years of routine flying, but the seconds in which everything is at stake. His handling of the emergency stood as a testament to the depth of his experience and the steadiness that only comes from a lifetime in the air.

Beyond the Cockpit

Wildlife Photography —
Australian Birds

Away from the demands of professional aviation, Neetheelesh finds solace and wonder in the forests of Hobart, Tasmania — spending his days off in quiet pursuit of Australia's remarkable birdlife. With cameras in hand and patience as his companion, he has developed a deep passion for wildlife photography, capturing the native birds of the Australian bush in their natural habitat.

It is a pursuit that mirrors his approach to flying — demanding stillness, acute observation, and an intimate understanding of the environment. The forests of Hobart have become his sanctuary, a world away from the cockpit yet no less demanding of focus and presence.

Virtual Skies

Microsoft Flight Simulator —
The Sky Never Sleeps

For a pilot of Neetheelesh's calibre, the love of flight does not end when the duty day is over. An enthusiastic devotee of Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024, he brings the same dedication to the virtual cockpit that he has always brought to the real one — meticulously setting up routes, monitoring weather, and flying with the discipline of a professional who knows no other way.

He is an active member of the VATSIM Network — the global online community where real-world procedures, live air traffic control, and thousands of virtual pilots converge to recreate the full experience of professional aviation. On VATSIM, callsign and professionalism matter, and Neetheelesh is at home in that world. For him, the simulator is not an escape from aviation — it is simply another expression of a lifelong devotion to the sky.
